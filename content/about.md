## How are you going to get all this done on your own?
The first major project I did on my own was anheuser-busch.com, and I rocked it! I know how to design, develop, talk to people and how to run a business. I don't need a team of (mis)management.

Here's the classic team based issue, and I know this because I've worked at agencies before. No one brings the developer to the meetings because that person is bad at communicating. Or the do, but the dev doesn't say anything or at least nothing that anyone understands. The designer is arrogant and thinks that their designs are the only thing that can save your business. And they scoff at you when you want a different font. The project manager is nice, but doesn't know anything about design nor development so this person is always needing to get back to you with an answer for your questions. And then there is a boss somewhere that is pushing the team to add as many services as possible because this extends the billable hour which makes the agency more money. I don't konw, maybe that's what you want. I'm just saying that I'm not here to waste your time or money.

## Why do you require 100% upfront?
I require 100% upfront because you are my bottleneck. While I have all day to work on your project, you have a full-time job on top of this project. So, I'm the one that has to wait for you to have time to communicate about the project. So, if we project that this will take 3 months to complete, but then something comes up at work and the project actually takes 6 months, then you are torching my payment schedule. So, when you pay me 100% up front, then you can take as long as you need to manage the project and no one needs to apologize for their time. And this leads into the next question below.

## Why don't you bill hourly?
I don't bill hourly because it's insane, especially in the area of design and development. Let's get real. You want an hourly rate because that's just how everyone works. But you want an hourly rate that isn't crazy so if the project runs long, then you wont be out of money. So, you want a firm deadline so you can keep projects from going over budget. But like above, how can we make a deadline, when your job demands more attention than our project? So, how cool are you going to be with me keeping the hourly bill running when you can't get back to me on time? You're not. And then all kinds of bad decisions get made in effort to rush the project to turn off the hourly charge before it gets too expensive. The billable hour is the root of all evil in a design/development project. So, I protect the project by giving you a FIXED cost. You will never pay a penny over that fixed cost. Now no one needs to rush. We can give the projected the time it needs to make informed decisions. But, I take on all the risk now. I protect the project and your pocketbook with a fixed cost. But if the project needs more time, then that's on me. This is why I require 10% upfront.

## How come you don’t do deadlines?
Like I said above, deadlines exist to for the purpose of protecting you from the madness of hourly billing. However, they ultimately sabotage the project and the working relationship between both parties. This is because all of the focus ends up being on managing the cost vs time balance and not on the effectiveness of the project. Now I know that there are special situations when something needs to be completed for a special event and that is understandable. But in that case, we can agree on a deadline, but then I will have the right to cut stuff out in order to meet that target date. Again, you are the project's bottleneck. I can't control your time, so I'm not going to bind the project to self-defeating practices. So, let's inhale a breath of fresh air and say, let's give this project the time it need to create as much value as possible for your business and it's patrons.
