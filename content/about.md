A redesign is a balance between three forces, what you want, what they want and how the game is played. Here's a high stakes example I had with Anheuser-Busch...

Years ago I redesign and redeveloped Anheuser-Busch.com alone. Internally, A-B wanted a fancy Flash website. Their only concern was with what they wanted. But hey, they were the king of beers. They could do what ever they wanted, right?

After all the internal decisions were made they came to me and said make this. I said that I could, but they were going to fail miserably and look like fools. They were shock and my words and asked why. I explained that Flash was a death wish. Mind you, this was when Flash was still in high demand.

Now here's a little history lesson. Apple didn't kill Flash with the release of the iPhone. Goolge killed Flash. And this is an even better time for a deeper history lesson. Flash is responsible for spreading the wild misconception that scrolling is bad. The Truth is that a Flash site couldn't be scrolled!  And instead of explaining this short coming, Flash design agencies instead sold Flash sites by claiming that scrolling was bad. It was the first time that things could be animated on a website, so most picked the hot new coolness of Flash at the expense of being able to scroll a website. And Flash sold like hot cakes because of this thurst for "interactivity." It was such a powerful time in web design that client are still requesting websites with no scrolling. In reality it's like asking for a book without pages.

But no one knew at the time was that Google couldn't read a Flash website. I know this is nerdy stuff, so let me try to make this easy to understand. Google reads text—not a movie. A flash site was really just a video that you could pause on certain frames. Let;s say that frame one was the home page and frame two was the about page. Cool, that's clever, but it's actually a movie that Google can't get text out. And Google indexes text based content. So if you choose Flash over HTML, then you are pretty much invisible to Google.

Do you think A-B wanted to be invisible on line? Do you think they wanted their SEO and rankings to disappear to their competitors? Hell no! So, years ago, I helped A-B dodge a HUGE online bullet and embarrassment because I knew how the game was played. So we made a website built to the latest standards of usability and SEO and launched a very successful redesign.

But don't let me forget to make this point in closing, SCROLLING IS NOT BAD! It's like saying that turning pages in a book is bad. This sounds silly right?! Scrolling is the nature on the webpage just like turning pages is the nature of a book or magazine. The point is to make the scroll and page turn worth it.  And making this minimal effort worth it is due to the magic of design, writing, imagery and usability.  So let's focus on these. I know you know what you want, but what do they want, and how can we play the game smartly? If we pay attend to these forces, we can make a bangin redesign.
